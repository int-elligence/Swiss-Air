    Neuenburg Vintage Festival

    Colourful days and nights

    Wine making in the mild climate of Lake Neuenburg dates back to the 10th century and is celebrated every year at the beginning of the traditional harvest. There is a children's procession, "Guggenmusik", costumed groups and a parade of decorated grape-harvest carts. Food and drink is also celebrated on the squares and alleys of Neuenburg's Old Town.

    Muleteers Festival Sbrinz Route

    Discover historic trading routes

    In the Middle Ages, caravans of muleteers transported goods across the Alps. Today, the cultures and customs of these historic trade routes are being rediscovered on walks and at muleteer festivals. The Sbrinz Route, named after the famous hard cheese of Central Switzerland, is where traditional crafts, original folk music and regional foods can all be experienced again today.

    Belle Époque Kandersteg

    Holiday like 100 years ago

    The "Belle Époque" refers to the period around 1900 when culture, business and society blossomed anew in Europe. It also coincides with the establishment of traditional winter tourism in Switzerland. Every year, the Belle Époque is celebrated for a whole week in the alpine resort of Kandersteg in the Bernese Oberland with a range of cultural, sporting and culinary highlights from the era.

    Fête de l’Escalade Genève

    Freedom since 1602

    On the night of 11/12 December, Geneva celebrates its victory over attempts by the Duke of Savoy's army to capture the city. According to the legend, Mère Royame joined the battle in 1602 – by dropping a large pot of hot soup on an enemy combatant. This is commemorated by the vegetable soup that is still as much a part of the festival today as the historic costumes and torch-lit procession.

    Castagnata Ascona

    Southern Switzerland's green gold

    Wood from the chestnut tree has always been a valuable building material for the people of Ticino, and for millennia its fruit was their primary source of food. Many Castagnetas like the one in Ascona take place to celebrate the era's "green gold". Apart from roast chestnuts, the culinary delights on offer also include honey, preserves and cakes, gnocchi, polenta and beer – all made from chestnuts.

    Schwägalp alpine wrestling

    National sport with an impressive mountain backdrop

    "Schwingen", a type of wrestling, is a traditional Swiss national sport. The annual "Schwägalp Schwinget" alpine wrestling festival is held at the foot of Säntis, the highest peak in the Alpstein mountains in Eastern Switzerland. At this popular event, the strongest wrestlers compete in a sawdust-strewn ring to win the main prize: a wreath, a breeding bull, fame and glory.

    Lötschental Tschäggättä

    Deliciously spooky carnival custom

    During the carnival season, residents of the Lötschen Valley in Valais dress up in wild costumes. Wrapped up in animal skins as "Tschäggättä" and wearing demonic wooden masks, their aim is to banish the winter. Or evil. Or both. This pagan custom is related to Alemannic rites, mixed with a touch of Catholic influences.
