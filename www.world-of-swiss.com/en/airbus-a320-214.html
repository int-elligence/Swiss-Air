• Airbus A320-214

    Flying with greater fuel-efficiency

    HB-IJB Montreux

    Named on 27 October 2007

    The airbus "Montreux" was christened during the Travel Trade Workshop TTW in Montreux. This traditional tourist destination, with its abundance of Belle Epoque charm, was not only home to Vladimir Nabokov and Charlie Chaplin, but also hosts the famous Montreux Jazz Festival.

    Close

    HB-IJQ Locarno

    Named on 18 August 2008

    The airbus "Locarno" was christened during the city's international film festival, at the Castello Visconteo close to the Piazza Grande, where for decades film has helped to shape Locarno's reputation as an important centre of tourism and culture. SWISS sponsors the event and is the festival's "Official Airline".

    Close

    HB-IJE Arosa

    Named on 24 June 2008

    The well-known Grisons tourist destination Arosa is one of Switzerland's holiday classics and for over 100 years has stood for hospitality both at home and abroad.

    Close

    HB-IJK Murten

    Named on 25 May 2011

    Two events in one: On 25 May 2011, the airbus "Murten" was christened and the SWISS Taste of Switzerland concept was presented at the Relais & Châteaux Hotel Le Vieux Manoir in Murten.

    Close

    HB-IJP Gstaad

    Named on 9 February 2012

    Gstaad is a sophisticated village in the municipality of Saanen in the Bernese Oberland. Or as actress Julie Andrews put it so succinctly: "Gstaad is the last paradise in a crazy world". And when this SWISS airbus was named after this renowned tourist destination, it was christened by none other than former ski racer and World Cup winner Michael von Grünigen.

    Close

    HB-IJX Bulle

    Named on 22 November 2012

    In order to symbolise the growing presence of SWISS in Romandy in Western Switzerland, the airline named an airbus A320 after Bulle. It is the main municipality of Gruyère in the French-speaking part of the canton of Freiburg. The aircraft was christened by Paralympic medalist Jean-Marc Berset, a resident of Bulle.

    Close

    HB-IJL Nyon

    Named on 30 November 2012

    Nyon on Lake Geneva, situated between Lausanne and Geneva, is based on Roman foundations, has the Paléo Open-Air Festival – and an airbus. The "Nyon" was christened by André Borschberg: CEO, co-founder and pilot at Solar Impulse, the Swiss research project for solar-powered aircraft. André Borschberg lives in Nyon.

    Close

    HB-IJN Crans-Montana

    Named on 13 December 2012

    The christening ceremony for the "Crans-Montana" was held in the Valais Alps. This spectacular event was attended by British actor and former "007", Sir Roger Moore. The "Crans-Montana" airbus pays tribute to the airline's presence in Western Switzerland and at Geneva airport.

    Close

    HB-JLT Grenchen

    Named on 25 October 2013

    The airbus "Grenchen" was christened at the airport of the same name, where Swiss Aviation Training trains the SWISS pilots.

    Close

    HB-IJM Richterswil

    Named on 1 December 2007

    Richterswil on Lake Zurich was home to the winner of a competition organised for the European Football Championship in 2008 in Austria and Switzerland. Former Swiss national trainer Köbi Kuhn was among the guests at the christening of the "Richterswil" airbus.

    Close

    HB-IJS Kloten

    Named on 23 June 2008

    It makes sense that Kloten, the site of SWISS's home airport Zurich, should lend its name to a SWISS airbus. Kloten is about 10 km from the centre of Zurich and is the main Swiss hub for international travel.

    Close

    HB-JLP Allschwil

    Named on 21 March 2011

    The municipality of Allschwil is situated right next to the city of Basel and the French border, just a few kilometres from the EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg. The airbus "Allschwil" pays homage to the many people who link Allschwil with the airport and with SWISS.

    HB-JLQ Bülach

    Named on 22 May 2011

    SWISS named its then newest airbus A320 "Bülach" to mark the occasion of the town's 1200-year anniversary. Bülach is the economic centre of the Zurich Unterland and a neighbouring municipality of Zurich airport. In terms of population Bülach is a small town and it is home to many SWISS pilots and flight attendants.

    Close

    HB-IJF Regensdorf

    Named on 4 September 2011

    Swissair Musik, founded in 1958 by members of staff, is now the "Swiss Band – Concert Band of SWISS International Air lines". The band entertained crowds at the 2011 Watterfäscht to mark the christening of the SWISS airbus "Regensdorf". Regensdorf is a small town near Zurich airport.

    Close

    HB-IJO Verbier

    Named on 27.2.2014

    The sponsors for Airbus "Verbier" were skiing legends Roland Collombin, Philippe Roux and William Besse of the Valais in Switzerland. The Valais tourist destination of Verbier and SWISS are a team, since guests from the UK and Scandinavia in particular like to travel SWISS when heading to Switzerland and Verbier for a holiday.
